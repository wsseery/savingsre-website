Why Coastal Insurance Matters
Before You Close

Florida's insurance market has hardened significantly — carrier exits, reinsurance cost increases, and Citizens restrictions mean you must secure coverage before closing, not after. High-value coastal properties require specialist underwriting.

Wind Coverage

Florida's coastal exposure requires standalone wind coverage — standard homeowners policies exclude wind damage in most coastal zones.

Flood Insurance

NFIP limits are $250K for structure. High-value properties require excess flood coverage from private carriers — often $1M–$5M in excess.

Umbrella & Portfolio

Multi-property investors need coordinated umbrella policies. AlphaGen structures coverage across your entire portfolio before any single closing.

Key Luxury Markets

SE Florida's luxury corridor runs from Jupiter Island south through Palm Beach, Boca Raton, Fort Lauderdale, and into Miami-Dade — each market with distinct character and pricing dynamics.

Highland Beach
Ultra-exclusive oceanfront enclave between Boca Raton and Delray Beach. Strict zoning limits high-rises — most properties are direct oceanfront or Intracoastal estates.
Manalapan
Palm Beach County's most exclusive barrier island municipality. Oceanfront-to-Intracoastal estates on a private island — among the most expensive per sq ft in Florida.
Jupiter Island
Martin County's ultra-wealthy enclave. Oceanfront estates with exceptional privacy, old-money character, and strict development controls preserving exclusivity.
Hillsboro Beach
Broward County's barrier island gem — the "Millionaire's Mile." Ocean-to-Intracoastal estates in a gated coastal corridor between Boca Raton and Deerfield Beach.
Boca Raton Oceanfront
Palmetto Park Rd to Spanish River — Boca's direct oceanfront corridor. Mix of legacy estates, modern builds, and luxury high-rises.
Palm Beach Island
The original Florida Gold Coast enclave. Ocean-to-lake estates on a barrier island with historic architecture and unmatched prestige.
